How to Make Traditional Lebanese Warak Enab

Learn how to prepare Warak Enab, a beloved Lebanese dish made with grape leaves stuffed with rice, herbs, and spices. This step-by-step tutorial walks you through the ingredients, rolling technique, and cooking tips to help you recreate this flavorful Middle Eastern classic at home.

Ingredients

  • 2 Potatoes, sliced
  • 1 Onion, sliced
  • 4 Medium sized carrots, sliced
  • 1/2 kg of grape leaves
  • 10 Mint leaves, chopped
  • Parsley, chopped
  • 2 Green onions, chopped
  • 7 Tomatoes, chopped
  • Rice, rinsed
  • 1/2 cup Olive oil
  • Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin
  • 3 Lemons

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. In a large bowl, mix rice with tomatoes, parsley, onion, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Lay out one grape leaf, vein side up. Place a spoonful of the rice mixture at the base of the leaf.
  4. Fold in the sides and roll tightly. Repeat with remaining leaves and filling.
  5. Place a few unrolled grape leaves at the bottom of a pot to prevent sticking.
  6. Arrange the stuffed grape leaves tightly in layers. Place a plate on top to hold them down.
  7. Add water and lemon juice until just covered. Simmer on low heat for about 45â€“60 minutes.
  8. Allow to cool slightly before serving. Warak Enab can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature.

Tips

  • For a meaty version, add ground beef or lamb to the rice filling.
  • Tastes even better the next day!
  • Serve with yogurt or a side salad for a full meal.
  • Enjoy!
